Analog Design Intern @ AMD

My 14-month internship at AMD completely steered my career into a new direction. I worked in the IP Characterization Team that characterizes the different internal and external analog IP that are used in new products. I mainly worked with the PCIe sub-team to test and improve AMD's PCIe 5.0 PHY. During my time there, I grew very familiar with various high bandwidth test equipment. Keysight UXRs, BERTs, VNAs, etc. are some of the bleeding-edge test equipment we had in the ESD controlled lab. I spent majority of my time either scripting test automation code which could be used to run mass testing for different conditions and settings, or debugging problems encountered with new PHYs behaving unexpectedly. I loved this role since every day was different. I acquired a completely new understanding of signal integrity and real-world transmission line theory for high-speed communication systems.
